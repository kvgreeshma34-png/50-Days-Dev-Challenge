Our Mission

Synexus is a peer-to-peer engineering community built and operated entirely by seniors at T John Institute of Technology under official institutional acceptance. Zero classroom lectures—just pure, student-led development focused on bridging the gap between theoretical knowledge and production-grade software engineering.

Active Initiatives

The 50 Days Web Development Challenge

A rigorous daily challenge guiding students through UI/UX, DOM logic, APIs, backends, and full-stack deployment. This intensive track focuses on real production workflows.

Open Source Engineering

Building modular components, optimizing performance pipelines, and contributing to reusable engineering toolkits across the stack.

Frequently Asked Questions

Who can join the Synexus community? +

Anyone passionate about tech, software engineering, and hands-on building! Open to all students eager to step up their development skills.

Is there any fee for joining or taking the 50-Days Challenge? +

No, Synexus is a 100% free, peer-led student engineering initiative.

How do I track and submit my daily progress? +

Commit your daily code updates directly to your GitHub repository and post your learnings on LinkedIn with #synexuscore.
